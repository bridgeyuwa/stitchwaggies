Moving abroad is stressful enough without worrying about the safety of your furry family members. Whether you're relocating to the UK, Canada, or the US, navigating Nigeria's export regulations requires precision, patience, and a bit of expert knowledge.

At Waggies, we've helped over 500 families successfully relocate their pets from Abuja to destinations worldwide. This guide breaks down the complex process into manageable steps, ensuring your pet arrives safely and happily at your new home.

1. Understanding Export Permits & Documentation

The most critical step in relocating your pet from Nigeria is securing the correct paperwork. Missing a single document can result in your pet being quarantined or, worse, denied entry at your destination.

lightbulb

Pro Tip: Start Early

We recommend starting the documentation process at least 3-4 months before your intended travel date. Rabies titers, in particular, can take weeks to process.

Essential documents typically include:

  • check_circle Import Permit: From the destination country (if required).
  • check_circle Export Permit: Issued by the Nigerian Agricultural Quarantine Service (NAQS).
  • check_circle Health Certificate: Issued by a licensed veterinarian within 10 days of travel.

2. Vaccination Requirements

Every country has specific health requirements. However, the Rabies vaccination is universal. Your pet must have a valid rabies certificate. For the EU and UK, a Rabies Titer Test (RNATT) is mandatory and must be done at an approved laboratory abroad, as there are no approved labs in Nigeria.

Our relocation specialists handle all permits, lab tests, and government endorsements for you.

3. Choosing the Right Travel Crate

Your pet will spend significant time in their crate, so comfort is non-negotiable. Airlines strictly enforce IATA Live Animals Regulations (LAR).

IATA Compliant Crate

Must be rigid plastic, secure metal bolts, and allow the pet to stand and turn around.

In-Cabin Carrier

Soft-sided carriers are often allowed for small pets (under 8kg) in the cabin.

4. Selecting Pet-Friendly Airlines

Not all airlines flying out of Abuja or Lagos accept pets. Lufthansa, Air France, and KLM have historically been the most reliable for international pet travel from Nigeria. They have temperature-controlled cargo holds specifically designed for live animals.

warning Heat Embargoes

Note that many airlines restrict pet travel during very hot months or times of day. Always check specific airline policies or consult with a relocation expert.
